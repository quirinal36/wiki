# School as Learning Hub

## Definition
School as Learning Hub is the idea that schools should stop acting primarily as knowledge monopolies and instead become places that guide, connect, and deepen learning across many environments.

## Why it matters
- Knowledge delivery is no longer scarce.
- Students can access high-quality learning resources outside formal classrooms.
- Physical schools remain valuable when they support guidance, experimentation, and high-bandwidth collaboration.

## Core functions
- Curate trustworthy outside resources.
- Build structures that reward [[learner-agency]].
- Create live discussion, interdisciplinary collaboration, and ethical reflection that AI alone cannot replace.
