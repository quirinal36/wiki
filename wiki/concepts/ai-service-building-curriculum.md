# AI Service-Building Curriculum

## Definition
AI service-building curriculum is an education model in which students use AI tools to design, build, ship, and explain real services instead of spending most of their time on memorization or isolated syntax drills.

## Why it matters
- It aligns classroom work with the actual AI-era skill mix: problem framing, tool use, iteration, and communication.
- It gives students a concrete artifact to show rather than only a test score.
- It supports [[learner-agency]] by making the student the owner of the idea, not just the consumer of instruction.

## Core ideas
- Start with a user problem or service idea.
- Use AI as a co-builder, not a replacement for thinking.
- Evaluate work by usefulness, clarity, and whether it can be shipped.
- Treat presentation and promotion as part of the build cycle.
- Teach students to specify what they want to build, what to change, and how to resolve blockers instead of only memorizing syntax.
- Even when AI can generate code, coding still has value as [[learner-agency]]-adjacent thinking practice.
