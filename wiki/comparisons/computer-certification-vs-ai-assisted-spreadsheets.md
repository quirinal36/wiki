# Computer Certification vs AI-Assisted Spreadsheet Work

## What is being compared
The source contrasts certification-style spreadsheet training with the reality of using AI directly inside office software.

## Why the comparison matters
It asks whether memorizing spreadsheet operations is still the right way to prepare students for real-world office work.

## Comparison table
| Dimension | Certification-first spreadsheet study | AI-assisted spreadsheet work |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Primary goal | Pass a test / earn a credential | Get work done efficiently |
| Main skill | Function recall and manual operation | Prompting, checking, and adapting |
| AI role | Outside the exam model | Built into the workflow |
| Student payoff | Familiarity with software basics | Faster practical output |
| Risk | Skills become outdated | Overreliance without understanding |

## Verdict / synthesis
The source does not argue that spreadsheets are useless. It argues that the educational center of gravity should shift from rote certification toward understanding features well enough to direct AI, cross-check output, and solve actual tasks. That fits the broader pattern in [[certification-track-vs-project-track]] and [[ai-off-workflow]].
