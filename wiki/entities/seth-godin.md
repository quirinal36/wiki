# Seth Godin

## Overview
Seth Godin is an author and strategist whose ideas are used in this vault as a lens for how people and organizations can remain valuable when AI commoditizes repeatable work.

## Why it matters
- The source ties his books "Linchpin" and "Tribes" directly to AI-era survival strategy.
- His framing helps translate abstract AI anxiety into practical questions about initiative, contribution, and community building.
- He is useful in this vault as a bridge between [[learner-agency]] and execution-oriented workflow thinking around differentiated value.

## Key facts
- "Linchpin" is used to argue for becoming indispensable through non-manualized contribution.
- "Tribes" is used to argue that committed communities matter more than shallow scale.
- His relevance here is not literary biography but operational positioning in an AI-shaped economy.
